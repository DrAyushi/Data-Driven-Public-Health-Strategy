###  Data-Driven Public Health Strategy for Early Detection & Cost Efficiency

---

##  Problem Statement

The district is facing a growing burden of hypertension, with significant gaps in early detection and access to care.

* 24% of adults are hypertensive
* 40% remain undiagnosed
* 60% of villages lack regular BP screening
* Stroke cases have increased by 18% in the last 5 years

👉 This leads to delayed treatment, higher hospitalization costs, and productivity loss.

---

##  Approach

A structured, data-driven strategy was developed combining:

* Demand–Supply Analysis
* Cost–Benefit Evaluation
* Workforce & Logistics Planning
* Phased Implementation Strategy

Two models were evaluated:

### Plan A: Full-Scale Rollout

* 80% population coverage
* High impact but high cost (~₹27–28 crore)
* Operationally intensive

### Plan B: Pilot + Phased Scale-Up

* 30% targeted coverage
* Lower cost (~₹8–10 crore)
* Better quality control and feasibility

---

##  Key Insights

* Benefit-Cost Ratio = **5.34** → Highly cost-effective
* Inpatient complications (e.g., stroke) drive major costs
* Rural and low-income populations face the biggest access barriers
* Workforce constraints limit scalability of full rollout
* Pilot model ensures better monitoring and data quality

---

##  Strategic Recommendation

👉 **Adopt Plan B (Pilot + Scale-Up)**

Because it:

* Reduces financial risk
* Improves implementation quality
* Allows system refinement before scaling
* Ensures sustainable long-term impact

---

##  Expected Impact

* ~5.4 lakh individuals screened (Year 1)
* ~52,000 new cases detected
* ~25,000 patients initiated on treatment
* Reduced stroke burden and hospital costs
* Improved health equity in underserved populations

---

##  Key Learning

This project demonstrates how **data, economics, and public health strategy** can be integrated to design scalable healthcare interventions.
